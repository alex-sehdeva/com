# Example UU Vision Statements

Real vision statements from UU congregations (and the UUA itself), gathered as reference for the COM and the drafting team. Useful for calibrating tone, length, and the kind of aspiration that lands well in a UU context.

A common observation: these are competent and effective, but many run together as variations on "welcoming + spiritual growth + justice." Worth reading them with an ear for what makes any single one *distinct*.

## Unitarian Universalist Association (UUA)

> A world transformed by compassion, equity, and love.

## All Souls Unitarian Church

> We are a community of seekers transforming ourselves and our world through love, justice, and service.

## First Unitarian Society of Madison

> We seek to be a welcoming, inclusive community, inspiring spiritual growth and working for a just and sustainable world.

## Unitarian Church of Montreal

> A caring, inclusive community, fostering spiritual growth and working for social justice.

## First Unitarian Church of Oakland

> We are a diverse, loving community, empowering one another to transform ourselves and the world.

## First Unitarian Universalist Church of Columbus, OH

> The vision of First Unitarian Universalist Church of Columbus is to transform and heal ourselves and our world through reason and love.

## Melrose UU Church, Melrose, MA

> We envision a dynamic faith community that nourishes our liberal religious values of love, reason, conscience, and action. We foster lives of integrity, joy, and service to each other, to Melrose and neighboring communities, and to the world.

## Unitarian Universalists of Clearwater

> An inclusive religious community, a beacon for reason, meaning and bold social action.

## UU Church of Palo Alto, CA — *What We Aspire to Be and Do* (long-form)

> We aspire to be a **caring** community that is welcoming, safe, inclusive, open-hearted, open-minded, and principled, which stewards our resources, nourishes our spirits, celebrates life events, and encourages us to become better versions of ourselves.
>
> We aspire to be a **learning** community open to evidence and insights from multiple sources, including our history, willing to engage in personal and community self-examination and exploration, and ready to acquire understanding and skills to strengthen our relationships, act effectively on our principles, and realize our aspirations.
>
> We aspire to be a **socially engaged** community working with others, with urgency, towards a just and compassionate society that respects and cares for our life-sustaining planet.

(Summary tag: "We aspire to be a Caring, Learning, and Socially Engaged spiritual community.")

## Outlier (non-UU, included for contrast)

**The Rock, San Diego, CA** (evangelical megachurch):

> Living out what we believe.

Five words. The shortest effective vision statement on record in the working materials — useful as a calibration point on how concise a statement can be while still functioning.
