## Decision 1 — Shape of the Oct 18 input service

### What the UUA literature presses for

- **Make it democratic and participatory, not delivered.** *"A shared vision, created among and by the people who are in the congregation"* enables real movement. *"Inviting the people into the process rather than just telling them the results"* is what produces commitment. (W/M/C, STICK)
- **Surface the gap, not just the dream.** ROLES introduces the concept of **"Accidental Values"** — habits and systems that quietly work against the stated vision. The example given: *"you have a vision for an intergenerational community, but you have people look askance at parents with babies."* The input service should be designed to *welcome* such tensions, not paper over them. (ROLES)
- **Listen for who isn't there.** *"Who is, and who is not, at the table"* — and *"what voices have been heard and what voices are missing."* The CIC guide is the most direct on this: women, people of color, LGBTQ+ individuals, and economically marginalized people have historically been *"left outside the covenant."* The Oct 18 service design must actively invite voices we wouldn't hear by default. (W/M/C, CIC)
- **Use small groups + whole-group sharing.** CIC recommends *"break out into groups of no more than six, with a timekeeper and note-taker"* and *"divide the time you have to allow for sharing back with the whole group."* This validates the dreaming-forward design (groups of 3–4 + whole-group harvest). (CIC)
- **Open and close ritually.** CIC: *"open with a reading and light a small chalice to set the intention."* Already in the dreaming-forward plan. (CIC)
- **Set a session covenant.** CIC: at the start of the gathering, agree explicit norms — *"Check for ableism, racism, ageism," "Everyone should have a chance to contribute," "Listen carefully,"* and the framing *"we are not making decisions or advocating for a 'side'; rather, we are sharing our thoughts."* Already in the dreaming-forward Appendix A but worth strengthening with the CIC language. (CIC)
- **Balance specificity and inclusivity in what we ask.** Vision should be *"specific enough to provide direction"* but *"broad enough to include multiple but complementary visions important to groups within the congregation."* Translation for the input service: prompts should be open enough that different parts of the congregation can answer them in their own register. (TIPS)
- **Design for "judgment and promise."** A strong vision contains *"both judgment and promise, by articulating the different future it wished to create."* Translation: at least one prompt should invite people to name what they want to *change*, not just what they want to keep. (TIPS)

### Implications for Tuesday

- We are not designing the order-of-service tonight. But the principles below should anchor the team's instincts about what the Oct 18 service needs to *produce* and *feel like*:
  - **Equity / who's at the table** — a supplementary-input channel for non-attendees is necessary, not optional.
  - **A "judgment" prompt** — at least one of the 2–3 questions (chosen in June) should invite people to name a tension or a thing-to-change.
  - **A strong session covenant** — borrow CIC's norm language about ableism/racism/ageism when we draft it.
  - **Small groups + whole-group sharing** is the format the UUA literature most directly endorses.

## Decision 2 — Composition of the drafting committee

### What the UUA literature presses for

- **Drafters are translators, not authors.** Their job is to render the congregation's *"core purpose"* — the shared aspiration — into language. They are also responsible for clarifying *"the promises you make to one another to 'stay at the table' during rough patches."* (ROLES)
- **The draft must be faithful.** The final statement must reflect *"the congregation's best understanding of its religious heritage."* This argues for at least one drafter who is steeped in CUUC's history and UU theology. (TIPS)
- **The draft must be inclusive.** *"Specific enough to provide direction"* and *"broad enough to include multiple but complementary visions"* — drafters need to be the kind of people who can *hear* difference and reflect it back without flattening. (TIPS)
- **Broad involvement is what makes mission/vision stick.** *"Involve most of the people in the congregation."* Even though the drafting committee itself is small, its *legitimacy* comes from being seen to draw faithfully on broad input. This argues for drafters with *visible standing* across different parts of the congregation. (STICK)
- **Address historical exclusions head-on.** Drafting team composition is a place where the *"who is missing from the table"* question is most concrete. (W/M/C, CIC)

### Implications for Tuesday

The UUA is opinionated about **what drafters need to do** but not prescriptive about **how to pick them**. That's a judgment call for the COM. The literature does suggest:

- **Size:** small. The dreaming-forward plan and Tricia's prep both land on **2–3 people**. Bigger groups draft worse.
- **Selection criteria** (synthesis from ROLES + TIPS + STICK + CIC):
  1. Skill with language (a writer / poet / teacher / preacher).
  2. Steeped in UU heritage and CUUC's particular history.
  3. Trusted across different segments of the congregation.
  4. Includes representation from voices that have historically been less heard.
  5. Available between Oct 18 and end of November.
- **Selection method:** the literature is silent. Reasonable to ask the COM team for their judgment.

## Decision 3 — Youth and intergenerational programming on Oct 18

### What the UUA literature presses for

The seven UUA pages are surprisingly **thin on direct youth-programming guidance** — only general gestures show up. But the gestures are pointed:

- **Generational renewal is intrinsic to mission work.** *"Each new generation must discover its own understanding of its purpose."* Implication: vision work that hands down conclusions from the existing generation has missed the point. (STICK)
- **Intergenerational community is exactly the kind of vision area where "Accidental Values" most often hide.** ROLES uses *"intergenerational community"* as its illustrative example — congregations claim it but don't always live it. If we don't actively design for youth voice in the input service, we are likely to perpetuate the gap. (ROLES)
- **The CIC guide assumes intergenerational participation by default,** in its small-group format. (CIC)

### Implications for Tuesday

- Including a parallel youth track on Oct 18 is **strongly aligned with UUA guidance**, even though no single page lays out a recipe. The dreaming-forward plan already includes age-appropriate materials and a simpler prompt — *"Draw or write what you wish our church could be like when you grow up."*
- **Decision points for Tuesday:**
  - Do we want a parallel youth visioning track (separate room, age-appropriate facilitation), or integrated into the main service with kid-friendly materials?
  - If parallel: who recruits/coordinates with the DRE? (4–6 weeks of prep typical.)
  - How do youth contributions get harvested into the same drafting input?

## Cross-cutting points worth surfacing

- **Vision is a living document.** *"Cannot just be done and put on a shelf"* — it must be *"used in the everyday life of the congregation."* (STICK) Reinforces COM's separate SPARC q3 desire to *institutionalize congregation document review*.
- **Refresh cycle.** Statements need revisiting *"at least every five years."* (STICK) — argues we should set our successor's expectations now (~2031 for a refresh).
- **Vision is "your congregation's vow with the universe, which is your theological understanding."** (ROLES) — useful framing language for the Oct 18 introduction.
- **Process takes time.** *"Developing and following a good process … is hard work and takes time."* (STICK) — push back gently if anyone proposes shortcuts.
- **One-page presentations are limited.** STICK in particular gestures at the Binghamton, NY case study but does not give details on the public site. Worth contacting **conglife@uua.org** if we want the full Binghamton materials. (STICK, TIPS)

## Where the UUA literature is weak (so we can't lean on it)

- **No detailed model for input-service design** beyond CIC's small-group recipe. Our best concrete model remains Palo Alto 2020 (see `prior_visioning_processes.md`).
- **No prescriptive selection method for drafters.** That decision is ours.
- **No youth-specific facilitation guidance.** We'll need to design that ourselves (or borrow from UU religious-exploration / OWL-style facilitation playbooks if we go parallel).
