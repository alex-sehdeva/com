# UUA Reference Resources

The canonical reading list COM has compiled for the vision statement project. Annotations summarize what each resource is most useful for.

## UUA LeaderLab — Core resources

- **[What Are Vision, Mission, and Covenant?](https://www.uua.org/leaderlab/what-are-vision-mission-and-covenant)**
  Definitions and the mental model of how the three documents interrelate. Start here if you've never thought about the distinction.

- **[Mission, Vision & Covenant: The Historical Context](https://www.uua.org/leaderlab/mission-vision-covenant-historical-context)**
  The covenantal tradition from the Cambridge Platform through contemporary UU practice. Good for grounding the work in UU heritage.

- **[The Roles of Mission, Vision and Covenant in Congregations](https://www.uua.org/leaderlab/roles-mission-vision-and-covenant-congregations)**
  Introduces the heart / arrow / oval / weight model and the concept of *accidental values* — habits and systems that work against the stated vision. Useful when surfaced themes reveal tension between aspiration and current practice.

- **[Why Undertake Vision, Mission, and Covenant Work?](https://www.uua.org/leaderlab/why-undertake-vision-mission-and-covenant-work)**
  The case for why non-creedal congregations must do this work explicitly. Useful for explaining the *why* to the congregation when introducing the process.

- **[Vision Statement Tips](https://www.uua.org/leaderlab/vision-statement-tips)**
  Quality criteria for effective vision statements. The most directly actionable single page for the drafting team.

- **[Making Mission & Vision Stick](https://www.uua.org/leaderlab/making-mission-vision-stick)**
  Binghamton, NY's experience with ministry action statements and ongoing assessment. Read this before adoption — it's about what happens *after* the vote.

## UUA Commission on Institutional Change (CIC)

- **[CIC Mission/Values Discussion Guide](https://www.uua.org/uuagovernance/committees/cic/institutional-change-congregation-resources/mission-values-discussion)**
  Framework for congregational truth-telling and transformation. Reminds us that vision work must consider the congregation's relationship to its surrounding community and to issues of equity — not only internal congregational life.
