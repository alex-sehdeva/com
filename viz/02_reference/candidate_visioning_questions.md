# Candidate Questions for Congregational Input

A bank of candidate questions to draw from when designing the Oct 18 input service (or any other input-gathering activity). Compiled by Tricia in the 2026-01-29 prep work.

**Per UUA guidance and Tricia's note: the facilitator should choose no more than three questions.** The point is depth, not coverage.

> *Answering these questions will inform the choices we make and the work of our committees and ministry, while reinvigorating our actions:*

1. What values, experiences and empowering strengths bring us together and evoke our care?
2. What future do we seek to create for ourselves as we engage with one another and our world?
3. What timeless, transcendent qualities of our religious community will we embody in all we do?
4. What new identity will build our reputation as "the _______ congregation"?
5. What is our ministry to people in the community outside the walls of our church?
6. What difference does this congregation mean to make in our denominational community?
7. What is our "brand"? How do we wish to be known by others in White Plains and surrounding communities?
8. What difference do we want worship to make in the lives of the variety of people who now come to us?
9. How will our congregation express the social justice principles that have led us to where we are currently? (Years ago we had no HOPE Kitchen, Refugee Resettlement, Democracy Matters, etc. — what can you envision in our future?)
10. How will we transform the lives of young people through our youth ministry? Older adults who choose to join us on the journey?

## Synthesis prompt (Palo Alto)

If we want a single overarching question to organize all of the input, the Palo Alto framing is strong:

> **What are we called to be and do?**

## Notes for selection

- Pair an **inward-facing** question (1, 3, 8) with an **outward-facing** question (5, 7, 9). The CIC Mission/Values guide warns against vision work that addresses only internal congregational life.
- Question 4 ("the _____ congregation") is high-leverage as a guided-imagery prompt — it forces specificity without prescribing the answer.
- Question 2 is broad enough to function as the single synthesis prompt for the guided imagery, with one or two more focused follow-ups.
