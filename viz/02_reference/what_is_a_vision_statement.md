# What Is a Vision Statement?

A short, shared reference to keep COM and the congregation aligned on what we are (and aren't) trying to produce.

## The three documents, distinguished

| Document | Question it answers | Time orientation |
|---|---|---|
| **Covenant** | How do we want to be in relationship with one another? | Present, ongoing |
| **Mission** | Who are we, and why do we exist? What do we do? | Present |
| **Vision** | Who do we want to become? What do we hope to achieve? | Future (5–10 years) |

CUUC has an existing covenant, mission, **Shared Values (2024)**, and **8th Principle (2021)** in addition to the (current) vision statement. The new vision should sit coherently alongside the other four documents and *add* what they don't already say. See [`current_cuuc_statements.md`](current_cuuc_statements.md).

A vision is **our dream, our vow with the universe** (UUA, *Unlocking the Power of Covenant*, June 2021).

## What makes a good vision statement

From UUA LeaderLab guidance:

- **Brief.** One sentence is the norm. Average length across UU congregations is ~15 words. The shortest effective statement on record is 5 words ("Living out what we believe").
- **Inspirational, clear, memorable, concise.**
- **Aspirational — not descriptive.** It says who we want to be, not who we are.
- **Faithful** to CUUC's religious heritage.
- **Future-oriented.** Not the present.
- **Contextual.** Appropriate to CUUC's size, resources, location, history.
- **Realistic** within our social context.
- **Specific enough to give direction, broad enough for inclusivity.**
- **Active verbs**, not passive or bureaucratic language.
- Contains both **judgment** (naming what needs to change) and **promise** (the future it envisions).

## Three things we are trying to draw out of the congregation

(From Alex's framing in the *Plan for Vision Statement Discussion*.)

1. **Attractor(s)** — what pulls us. A felt sense of "more just," "more whole," "more alive." Not always defined, but recognizable.
2. **Movement** — how we engage. Growth, questioning, repair, transformation. Often continuous, not completed.
3. **Recognition** — how we know it's ours. People see themselves in it. It resonates across differences. It feels like *us*, not imposed.

## A working definition

A vision statement is:

- a **shared orientation toward becoming** — the thing a group can point to and say, "That is the direction we recognize ourselves moving in, together."
- a **collective expression** that names what matters, how we change, and what we are moving toward, without closing off how it must be said.
- a **container for a group's future-facing identity**, spacious enough to include many voices, specific enough to guide real choices.
