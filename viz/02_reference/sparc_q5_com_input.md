## Important framing note

This document is **COM's own collective input to SPARC**, not the broader congregation's responses. SPARC has separately gathered (or is gathering) input from many groups across CUUC; that wider data has not yet been shared with COM. When that data arrives we should treat it as the primary congregational signal. **For now, this PDF tells us what COM as a group already sees** — useful for designing the input service (so we surface the same substance from the wider congregation), and useful as a reality check on whether our process is hearing voices beyond our own.

## Question 5 — "Reach for the sky" vision (the directly relevant one)

> *In a "reach for the sky" vision for the next 5–10 (or even 20–40) years, what would you hope for CUUC?*

The question is split into three sub-prompts. Below are the themes COM raised, lightly grouped.

### a) TO BE DOING

- **Summer school enrichment program.**
- **Local outreach to UU college students** on nearby campuses.
- A resource-allocation principle: **½ operations / ¼ discernment / ¼ mobilization** — i.e. half of CUUC's resources delivering on existing commitments, a quarter experimenting with new ones, a quarter responding to urgent situations.

### b) TO BE ABLE TO DO

- **Robust youth group.**
- **Shelter for the homeless.**
- **Sanctuary shelter for refugee families** — including potentially purchasing another property for refugee housing.
- **Respond to dynamic situations in visible numbers.**
- **Faithfully deliver on a defined set of commitments.**
- **Help people participate smoothly.**

### c) WHO WILL WE BE

- **An expanded version of who we are.**
- **A known public voice** in moments of crisis: *"Well, you know CUUC will have something to say about this!"* — comparable in posture to Sharpton, ADL, NAACP.
- **Locally known as the place to go for:**
  - The power of authentic and human-centered community.
  - Living authentically with open minds and hearts.
  - Freedom from creeds beyond a belief in each person's value and dignity.
  - **Prizing compassion *and* debate** (note the "and" — both, not either/or).
  - Authentic, challenging, compassionate, human-centered activities.
  - Being welcomed and treated with dignity.
  - Supporting democratic institutions.

## Themes that emerge from Q5

Reading the three sub-answers together, four implicit themes show up that are worth holding lightly during the input service design:

1. **Outward-facing public moral voice** — CUUC as a known local actor, not just an internal community.
2. **Concrete service infrastructure** — homeless shelter, refugee housing, summer enrichment — substantive, not symbolic.
3. **Intergenerational continuity** — robust youth group, college-student outreach, summer programming for kids.
4. **A culture of authenticity + debate** — "compassion *and* debate," "open minds and hearts," "free from creeds." This is non-trivial — it implies a vision that explicitly welcomes difference and disagreement, not just warmth.

## Adjacent context from Q1–Q4

These were not vision-framed questions, but two themes show up that bear on vision work:

- **Document discipline.** COM explicitly wants to *"institutionalize congregation document review: Vision, Mission, and Covenant"* — i.e. the work we are doing now should produce not just a statement but a recurring practice. (Q3 — *"WANTS"*.) Also: *"commit to n-year updates per document"* (Q4 — *"STRATEGIES"*).
- **Information-gathering hygiene.** COM wants a "Survey Budget" and a policy around "ambient information gathering." Implication: the Oct 18 service should be designed with awareness that the congregation may already be feeling survey fatigue (SPARC has been gathering input concurrently).

## Implications for the Oct 18 input service

- **Ensure outward-facing prompts are present.** Q4 themes (a) and (c) are very outward-facing; an input service that only asks "what does worship feel like" will under-sample this.
- **Make space for intergenerational signal.** Either dedicated youth programming, or prompts that explicitly ask about youth / older adults / families.
- **Don't suppress tension.** "Compassion *and* debate" is a productive tension; the input service should welcome dissenting and surprising responses (the dreaming-forward "Unique Themes" sheet does this well).
- **Acknowledge the SPARC context.** The congregation has just been asked many questions by SPARC. Frame the Oct 18 input service as a *different* kind of asking — generative and visionary, not diagnostic — so it doesn't read as more of the same.
