# CUUC Current Statements

The five things CUUC publicly publishes that describe what it stands for. All sourced from [cuucwp.org/worship/our-values-beliefs](https://cuucwp.org/worship/our-values-beliefs/) (last fetched 2026-05-03), except where noted.

The vision statement is the one being **replaced** by this project. The other four are stable references the new vision should sit alongside coherently.

## Mission Statement

> Nurture each other in our spiritual journeys, foster compassion and understanding within and beyond our community, and engage in service to transform ourselves and our world.

*A mission statement names **who we are and why we exist** — what we do.*

## Vision Statement (current — **to be replaced**)

> Community Unitarian Universalist Congregation at White Plains will be a welcoming "sanctuary without walls" that promotes diversity, fellowship, spiritual growth, and inspiration, while committing to people and the planet through social action and service.

**Note from Tricia's prep work:** this statement reads as a description of who we are *now*, rather than who we envision ourselves becoming. Vision statements per UUA guidance should be **future-facing** — a 5-to-10-year aspiration. That observation is part of the motivation for the rewrite.

## Community Covenant

> With open hearts and minds,
> we, the community of CUUC, children, youth, adults, elders, all, covenant together to shape our congregation on a foundation of love, acceptance, kindness, and respect.
>
> Strengthened by our common humanity,
> we strive to be welcoming, inclusive, and cultivate a sense of belonging. We embrace diversity as a source of strength, honoring the gifts we each bring.
> We can disagree respectfully and be open to change, understanding that people meet with different abilities, backgrounds, experiences, and expectations.
>
> Sharing a vision of being in relationship with one another,
> we listen to others with curiosity and compassion, and we provide space for the quiet to be heard. We support one another in times of joy and in times of struggle. And we make time for rest and renewal.
>
> Working side by side,
> we strengthen our community as we engage in acts of service. We acknowledge our vulnerabilities while building trust among ourselves,
> enabling us to work together toward justice in our world. We participate and pledge as we can to support our community.
>
> Inspired to explore life's questions and mysteries,
> we affirm and kindle our connectedness. We foster the journeys of our congregants across generations while in the embrace of community.
>
> Honoring our interdependence,
> we hold ourselves accountable to each other by earnestly engaging in dialogue, both to understand and to be understood. When we fall short of our covenant, we seek to reconcile with one another with patience and grace. When needed, we ask for support from our minister and congregation.
>
> We hold ourselves to these ideals, and each day, we begin again with love.

*A covenant describes **how we want to be in relationship** with one another — how we do things together. It is distinct from the mission (what we do) and the vision (who we want to become).*

## Shared Values (adopted 2024)

The six UUA shared values, adopted by CUUC in 2024:

- **Interdependence**
- **Equity**
- **Transformation**
- **Pluralism**
- **Generosity**
- **Justice**

…with **Love** at the center (per UUA framing).

## 8th Principle (adopted 2021)

> Journeying toward spiritual wholeness by working to build a diverse multicultural Beloved Community by our actions that accountably dismantle racism and other oppressions in ourselves and our institutions.

## What this means for the visioning process

When the drafting team works on candidate statements, the new vision should sit *coherently alongside* these other four documents — not duplicate them, not contradict them.

- The **Covenant** already names how we relate (welcome, respectful disagreement, accountability, reconciliation, intergenerational care).
- The **Mission** already names what we do (nurture, foster compassion, engage in service).
- The **Shared Values** and **8th Principle** already name the substantive commitments (interdependence, equity, transformation, pluralism, generosity, justice; anti-racism and Beloved Community).

The **vision** should answer the remaining question: *what becomes possible if we live all of the above for 5–10 years? Who do we become?* It should be future-facing, aspirational, and add something the other four documents don't already say.

> **Drafting team caution:** if a candidate vision statement reads as a paraphrase of the Mission or Covenant, it isn't doing its job.
