# CUUC Vision Statement Project — Overview

> For the strategic spine — how UUA's recommended process maps onto our meeting allocations and our specific failure-mode mitigations — see [`process_strategy.md`](process_strategy.md).


## What we're doing

The Committee on Ministry (COM) is leading a democratic process to **update CUUC's vision statement** and bring a new statement to a congregational vote in late 2026.

The current statement reads as descriptive of who CUUC is *now*. A vision statement should be **future-facing** — a 5-to-10-year aspiration for who the congregation wants to *become*.

## Scope and constraints

- **Democratic and participatory**, but structured enough to avoid large-scale open-ended drafting.
- **Existing source material** is treated as meaningful input — we are not starting from a blank page. CUUC already publishes a mission, the current vision, a community covenant, the six UUA Shared Values (adopted 2024), and the 8th Principle (adopted 2021) at https://cuucwp.org/worship/our-values-beliefs/. SPARC findings, when available, will add congregational signal. The new vision should sit alongside the other four CUUC documents and *add* what they don't already say.
- **Aligned with SPARC.** Bill Roberts is leading SPARC, which is delivering a draft report to the Board late summer 2026 and aiming for its own congregational vote in late autumn. CUUC's vision-statement vote may piggyback on that timing. SPARC's "question 5" is expected to be a key input to our process when released.
- **Summer is mostly dark.** COM does not meet for ~3 summer months. The realistic working months are May, June, September, October, November, December.

## The process (agreed at 2026-04-07 COM meeting)

1. **Gather and organize existing source material.** UUA LeaderLab resources, Tricia's prep work, the current CUUC statements, and (when available) SPARC's findings.
2. **Run a congregation-wide input event on Sunday 2026-10-18.** This is the main input gathering and is taken as given. Specific format (worship vs. hybrid vs. workshop-in-worship's-clothes) and supplementary input channels are TBD. A working strawman service plan exists in `04_workshop_design/` as Alex's internal reference, not as a team-facing proposal.
3. **Small drafting group synthesizes themes and produces candidate statement(s).** "Congregational poets" — 2–3 people skilled with language, representing different parts of the congregation.
4. **Bring candidates back to the congregation** for at least one round of structured feedback and revision.
5. **Final statement to a congregational vote** by end of 2026, ideally bundled with the SPARC vote.
