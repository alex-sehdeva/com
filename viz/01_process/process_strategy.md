## Mapping UUA's stages to our reality

| 2. Understanding the change process | **Light touch.** Keep Hobgood's seven stages of resistance in mind; brief the team but do not over-formalize. |
| 3. Selling the process | **Partial.** Board chair + minister aligned via Apr 5 email. **We have not yet engaged informal influencers.** Action: dedicate explicit time in June to identify and engage them. |
| 4. Planning the process | **In flight.** This document and the timeline are the plan. |

| 6. Creating the mission | **Skip.** CUUC has one and it stays. |

| 10. Revisiting | UUA recommends every 5 years; document in the adoption package so the next refresh is foreseeable (~2031). |

## Failure modes (from the booklet) and how we mitigate

The UUA booklet is opinionated about how vision projects fail. Mapped to our project:

### 1. "Words on paper" — the rolled-up newsprint in a closet

> *"The reason why most vision, mission, and covenant statements fail is because the congregation fails to plan for their effective implementation."* — p.59

**Mitigation:** Carve out **explicit November + December COM time** for follow-through planning, *before* the vote. Components: liturgical use plan, committee-goals integration, mission-objectives draft (3–4 SMART objectives), budget integration ask for the next pledge cycle, periodic-revisit calendar (~2031). The new vision should arrive at the vote with its life-after-adoption already sketched.

### 2. Skipping the "selling" stage → late resistance

> *"If key individuals are boycotting the meetings or speaking against them. If this happens, take a step back and do more of the marketing work."* — p.27

**Mitigation:** In June, identify the ~10–15 informal influencers (former board members, deeply respected long-tenured members, committee chairs we haven't yet engaged). Plan touchpoints with them *before* publicity for Oct 18. The board has signed off; the unofficial leadership has not yet been brought in.

### 3. Treating adaptive change as technical

> *"It may be tempting to simplify these kinds of issues and identify them as needing a technical solution."* — p.25

**Mitigation:** Push back when conversation shifts to "we just need to pick three questions and run a service." The hard work is asking *why* CUUC wants to be what it wants to be. Make sure the Oct 18 questions are adaptive (about meaning, identity, tension), not technical (about formats, programs).

### 4. Insufficient breadth of input

> *"Permit enough time to engage the widest possible participation … include a variety of opportunities for input and involvement."* — p.28

**Mitigation:** Oct 18 + supplementary-input channel + youth track. **Do not stop there.** Consider in June whether to add: a quiet-table / reflection-cards station outside Sunday services in September–October, a dedicated friends-and-newer-members touchpoint, an explicit invitation to less-active members.

### 5. Not making space for quieter voices

> *"Help provide opportunities for quieter persons to be involved. One simple way is to have a period of silence for reflection and individual journaling following each question so that those who need the space have a chance to think before the more talkative people fill up the silence."* — p.27

**Mitigation:** Build silence and journaling into the Oct 18 service design. The dreaming-forward strawman already does this; preserve it in any redesign.

### 6. Skipping young people

> *"Don't assume that the adults have all the good ideas!"* — p.30

**Mitigation:** Decision target on 5/5: parallel youth track on Oct 18, with an integrated harvest path back to the drafting team. (Tip from booklet, p.35: have intergenerational sharing groups so adults capture youth words as they explain drawings.)

## Drafting team — what the booklet specifically says

From p.20 (team-selection, applies to drafters):

- Open to new ideas, work well with others, well-respected.
- Can work in consensus; capable of self-assertion *and* compromise.
- **The minister can be on the drafting team but probably should not chair it** — "the professional leadership often have enough on their plates."
- Balanced across areas of interest, longevity of membership, leadership experience.

This is consistent with what we've already pulled together; bring it forward into the May 5 / June discussion.

## What we are deliberately *not* doing

- **Full UUA process** — we are doing vision only, not VMC together. CUUC's mission and covenant remain.
- **Multiple major input events** — we anchor on Oct 18 + supplementary channels rather than running a Palo-Alto-style two-workshop sequence. Time doesn't allow.
- **Mission objectives during this project** — we sketch them as a *follow-through* deliverable, but the actual SMART objectives are a 2027 deliverable for the next chair.
- **Outside facilitator** — UUA mentions this option (p.32). We have not pursued it; if Oct 18 logistics start to threaten, this is the lever to consider pulling.

## Anchor commitments

What we will not compromise on, even if other things slip:

1. **Adaptive, not technical, framing** — vision questions stay about *who CUUC becomes*, not *what CUUC does next year*.
2. **Breadth of voice** — Oct 18 is the main but not the only input channel.
3. **Follow-through plan exists at the vote** — not "we'll figure that out later."
4. **The vision sits coherently alongside CUUC's existing mission, covenant, Shared Values, and 8th Principle** — does not duplicate them.
5. **The drafting team is small (2–3) and selected for skill *and* trust across the congregation.**
6. **Intergenerational input is real, not gestural.**
